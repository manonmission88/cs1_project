The Hobbit Review

By: Bob Nelson, April 20, 2022

As an almost 65 years old crouton, I’m positively enchanted by this pre “lord of the rings” series which is and always be in my top 10 best adventure and fantasy books ever written (and most faithful and to the point turned into Hollywood movie trilogy as thrilling to watch on big screen as to read on the books). Kudos to Tolkien who deserves a very special place in the pantheon of best writers ever.

To Kill a Mocking Bird

By: Reginald Hilliard April 2, 2014

There are numerous parallel story lines in this story told through the perspective of our six year old protagonist, Scout. The most important being the fascination with social recluse, "Boo" Radley, by Scout, her brother Jem, and their friend Dill, along with the very contrasting trial of Tom Robinson, a black man who is accused of raping a white woman, with Atticus, the children's father, as the appointed defendant. Both these storylines come together in the end after long, separate journeys with shared motifs and symbols that lead to the story’s most prevalent theme surrounding the topic of innocence.
